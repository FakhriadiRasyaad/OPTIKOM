Module Content

In this module, we will explore the fundamental concepts of optical fiber technology, focusing on the measurement of Numerical Aperture (NA). Through hands-on activities, participants will learn how to measure the NA of optical fibers and compare the characteristics of Single Mode Fiber (SMF) and Multimode Fiber (MMF). Additionally, the module introduces the structure of optical fibers, detailing the core, cladding, and coating layers, and how these components affect the fiber's performance. This knowledge is essential for understanding the principles of light transmission in fiber optics.

Preliminary Task

Module Content

In this module, participants will learn the fundamental techniques for measuring fiber optic networks using two essential tools: the Optical Time Domain Reflectometer (OTDR) and the Optical Power Meter (OPM). The OTDR will be used to measure attenuation, detect faults, and identify the location and causes of damage within fiber optic cables. Additionally, participants will be trained on how to utilize the OPM to measure signal strength and assess attenuation in fiber optic systems, ensuring proper performance and maintenance of the network.

Module Content

In this module, participants will learn how to perform fiber optic splicing using a fusion splicer, a crucial technique in ensuring minimal signal loss in fiber optic networks. The process will cover key stages including stripping the fiber, cleaning it to remove any contaminants, cleaving it for precise cuts, and finally, performing the splicing using the fusion splicer to fuse the fibers together. This hands-on practice will help participants master the steps required to create high-quality, low-loss fiber optic connections.
